Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square, located in the ancient city of Bhaktapur, is another UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Kathmandu Valley. Known for its well-preserved medieval architecture, historical significance, and vibrant culture, Bhaktapur Durbar Square is one of the most beautiful and historically rich sites in Nepal. The square has long been the center of politics, culture, and religion in Bhaktapur and remains an important destination for tourists and locals alike.

Historical Significance
Bhaktapur Durbar Square dates back to the 12th century and was developed as the royal palace complex during the Malla period. Bhaktapur, once the capital of the Malla kingdom, was a significant political, religious, and cultural center in the Kathmandu Valley. The square is a testimony to the city’s rich heritage and remains one of the finest examples of traditional Nepali architecture.

Major Attractions
Bhaktapur Durbar Square is home to a remarkable collection of temples, shrines, courtyards, and sculptures. Some of the major attractions include:
- Vatsala Temple: A stunning temple dedicated to Goddess Vatsala, famous for its intricate carvings and peaceful atmosphere.
- Palace of 55 Windows: The most famous building in Bhaktapur Durbar Square, this magnificent wooden structure has 55 windows and is known for its exquisite craftsmanship. It was once the royal palace of the Malla kings.
- Vishnu Temple: A significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, featuring a beautifully crafted shikhara-style architecture.
- Bhairabnath Temple: A historic temple dedicated to the fearsome god Bhairab, known for its imposing appearance and intricate woodwork.
- Nyatapola Temple: One of the tallest temples in Nepal, Nyatapola is a five-story pagoda-style temple dedicated to Goddess Siddhi Lakshmi.
- Statue of King Bhupatindra Malla: A statue of the king who greatly contributed to the construction and beautification of Bhaktapur Durbar Square.
- Gopi and Pashupatinath Temple: Temples dedicated to the Hindu deities, with beautiful carvings and architecture.
- Golden Gate: The entrance to the 55 Windows Palace, a golden gate adorned with beautiful images of Hindu gods and goddesses.

Festivals and Events
Bhaktapur Durbar Square is the focal point for numerous cultural and religious festivals throughout the year. Some of the main celebrations include:
- Indra Jatra: A grand procession featuring chariot festivals, traditional dances, and the presence of the Living Goddess Kumari.
- Bhaktapur Dhan Naach: A traditional dance performed by the people of Bhaktapur, held during special festivals and celebrations.
- Dashain: The biggest Hindu festival in Nepal, celebrated with rituals and ceremonies throughout the square.
- Tihar: A festival of lights and offerings, with celebrations centered around the worship of animals, especially cows, crows, and dogs.
- Gai Jatra: The “cow festival,” where people participate in a parade with decorated cows and costumes.

Earthquake Damage and Restoration
Bhaktapur Durbar Square, like the other Durbar Squares in the Kathmandu Valley, was severely damaged by the 2015 earthquake. Many of the temples, including the Vishnu Temple and parts of the Palace of 55 Windows, suffered damage. However, the restoration and reconstruction work is ongoing, with many structures already restored to their former glory with the help of the Nepali government and international organizations.

Tourism and Importance
Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a major tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world who come to admire its traditional architecture, religious significance, and vibrant culture. In addition to the historical sites, the square is surrounded by local markets, pottery shops, cafes, and traditional handicraft stalls, where visitors can experience the lifestyle and craftsmanship of the locals.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a treasure trove of Nepali history, culture, and architecture. It is a symbol of Bhaktapur’s glorious past and a living testament to the artistic and religious practices of the Malla era.
The square’s beauty, cultural richness, and historical significance make it one of Nepal’s most cherished and visited landmarks. A visit to Bhaktapur Durbar Square offers a fascinating glimpse into the heritage of the Kathmandu Valley and the vibrant life of its people.
